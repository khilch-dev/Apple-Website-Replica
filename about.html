About Apple.

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. It was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne to develop and sell Wozniak’s Apple I personal computer. The company went public in 1980 and has since become one of the world’s largest technology companies by revenue. Apple designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, computer software, and online services. Its hardware products include the iPhone, iPad, Mac, iPod, Apple Watch, and Apple TV. Its software products include the macOS and iOS operating systems, the iTunes media player, the Safari web browser, and the iLife and iWork creativity and productivity suites. Apple’s online services include the iTunes Store, the iOS App Store, and Mac App Store, Apple Music, and iCloud. As of March 2023, Apple is the world’s largest company by market capitalization, and with US$ 383.29 billion the largest technology company by 2023 revenue. Apple has 161,000 employees worldwide and operates 527 retail stores.
